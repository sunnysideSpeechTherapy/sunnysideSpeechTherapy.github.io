Sunnyside Speech Therapy:
Therapeutic Philosophy and Modalities

A Pediatric Speech-Language Pathology Private Practice providing a positive and supportive environment for assessment and treatment of speech, language and feeding/swallowing disorders. Sunnyside certified therapists utilize a comprehensive child-centered approach incorporating movement, music, sensory integration and play in a fun, productive and purposeful way to help a child reach their communication needs.

If your child has shown signs of speech delays, receptive or expressive language delays, feeding delays with intake or swallow, oral motor deficits, or has been diagnosed with autism, cerebral palsy, down syndrome, developmental delay, prematurity, or any other genetic or neurological disorder, please contact us for a free consultation to see if SunnySide Speech Therapy can help your families needs!

Play

Play is an essential and critical part of all children's development. Play is how children learn to socialize, to think, to solve problems, to mature and most importantly, to have fun. A child's exposure to play provides positive influences on the development of fine and gross motor skills, language, speech, socialization, listening and attention, emotional well-being, creativity, problem solving and learning ability. Play is a foundation philosophy and learning modality at Sunnyside Speech.  

Movement

The body is a tool to increase learning through doing, moving, acting and interacting. Movement can influence the efficiency of the brain’s function and can accelerate a child’s learning process. There is correlation that when learners are actively engaged with moving their own bodies that their performance and memory is greatly improved and long lasting.  

Along with movement, sensory integration is a foundational concept to creating an optimal learning environment. Individual children’s sensory systems are taken into consideration to increase arousal, regulation, modulation, and overall engagement to maximize learning potential. At Sunnyside speech therapy children are given opportunities for jumping, bouncing, rolling, dancing and marching just to name a few examples of possibilities pairing movement and sensory integration with speech and language objectives.

Music

Kids love listening, moving and singing along with familiar music at school, in the car, on video games, movies and TV. Research demonstrates that exposure to music from early age helps children to speak more clearly, develop a larger vocabulary, and strengthen social and emotional skills. Music has the ability to strengthen the connection between the body and brain and enhance all whole body development. At SunnySide Speech, kids will be exposed to music throughout ambience background entrainment, rhythmic training for regulation, blowing whistles for feeding and speech production coordination, singing for learning and remembering concepts and self regulation, marching and moving for coordination, and exposure to various vibrational environments for sensory exploration.

NDT: Nuero-Developmental Training

​Rehabilitation approach utilizing movement, posture, sensorimotor processing and reflex techniques with individuals with neurological conditions.

S.O.S.: Sequential Oral Sensory Approach to Feeding

​The SOS approach uses a multidisciplinary approach for assessment and treatment for children with feeding difficulties that integrates posture, motor coordination, sensory processing, oral motor skills and behavioral, medical and nutritional individual factors to manage feeding and growth problems.

SRJ Oral motor therapy (TalkTools)

​Oral motor: a Tactile-Sensory approach to oral motor based speech production delays.

Beckman Oral motor Intervention and Assessment

Oral motor intervention to provide assisted movement to improve coordination and strength for speech, feeding and swallowing safety.

RMTi: Rhythmic Movement Training International

A movement based primitive reflex integration program that uses developmental movements and isometric pressure to rebuild the foundations to assist in learning, sensory, emotional and behavioral challenges for children and adults.
